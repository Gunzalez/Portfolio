BUPA (/bupaTV)

And here we have something different, a web site for TV. While working at Bupa I was lucky enough to have a go at making a web site for TV and quite an interesting little project it was.

Web sites of TV are made a little different from those for computers, consideration for the auidence and input/ouput devices play an important part of design. The fact that people sit further away from television monitors than they do computer screens for example, will affect page layout.

TV monitors also run at a different resolution to computer screens and while developing I had to simulate this using an Emulator, sadly I never got to see this on actual TV as I don't subscribe to ntl.

Another difference it that TV's don't usually come with keyboards and the JavaScript type language was used to respond to Remote Control button presses.

I've not used this knowledge since but it was good to be involved, and this could spring ideas for the future, it's amazing what one can do with this aspect of the web, more information can be obtained from www.digitalcabletv.co.uk/.

An interesting project and an area where not too many compaies have a presence, it's clearly a medium favoured by coporate organisations as I noticed all the banks such as Barclays, Llyods, Natwest and superstores like Argos and Ikea had web sites for TV already, and you don't get more coporate than Bupa.

Web sites like these serve as more effective ways of advertising, but only when the new wave of TV technology catches on, how many people today have or even use web for TV today, very few.
